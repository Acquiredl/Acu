# FAIR Principles

**Concern:** Making digital research assets Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable.
**Issuing body:** GO FAIR (multi-stakeholder consortium); peer-reviewed origin.
**Scope:** Four pillars decomposed into sub-items (F1–F4, A1–A2, I1–I3, R1–R1.3), each individually citable.

## When to cite

Cite when a pipeline emits or curates research data. Pick a specific sub-principle (e.g., "FAIR F1 — persistent identifiers"), not just "FAIR".

## Notes

De-facto standard for grant-funded science. Endorsed by NIH, EU Horizon Europe, G7 Open Science. No competing formal ISO standard for data stewardship. Peer-reviewed origin gives academic authority despite the de-facto label.
