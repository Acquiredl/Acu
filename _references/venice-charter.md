# Venice Charter 1964

**Concern:** Foundational principles for conservation, restoration, and archaeological practice — authenticity, reversibility, documentation, evidence-based intervention.
**Issuing body:** ICOMOS.
**Scope:** Sixteen articles establishing professional norms for heritage work; implemented by UNESCO World Heritage guidelines.

## When to cite

Cite when a pipeline touches built heritage, monument conservation, or archaeological intervention. Key citable articles: Article 3 (restoration stops where conjecture begins); Article 16 (precise documentation requirement).

## Notes

1964 adoption; Modernist perspective. The Nara Document on Authenticity (1994) updates the charter for culturally-pluralist contexts and is a common companion citation. No paywall. Doctrinal charter, not a measurable standard — use for framing, not compliance claims.
